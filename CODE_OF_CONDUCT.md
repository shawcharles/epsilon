# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

Epsilon is an open-source statistical software project. The project should be a
professional, respectful, and technically serious environment for discussing
Bayesian marketing mix modelling, Julia implementation details, documentation,
examples, bugs, and research-software practice.

## Expected Behaviour

Participants are expected to:

- communicate respectfully and constructively;
- focus criticism on ideas, evidence, code, documentation, and modelling
  assumptions rather than on individuals;
- be clear about uncertainty, limitations, and reproducibility concerns;
- respect differences in experience level, disciplinary background, and
  modelling workflow;
- disclose relevant conflicts of interest when they affect a technical or
  methodological discussion.

## Unacceptable Behaviour

Unacceptable behaviour includes:

- harassment, intimidation, threats, or sustained personal attacks;
- discriminatory language or conduct;
- sexualised language or imagery;
- deliberate disruption of issues, discussions, or reviews;
- publishing private contact information or private communications without
  permission;
- bad-faith misrepresentation of the project, its contributors, or reported
  results.

## Reporting

To report a concern, contact Charles Shaw at
<charles@charlesshaw.net>. Reports should include enough context to understand
what happened, where it happened, and who was involved.

Reports will be reviewed with attention to confidentiality, proportionality, and
the safety of affected participants. Possible responses include clarification,
moderation, closing or locking discussions, removing comments, blocking
participants from project spaces, or escalating to platform maintainers.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to project spaces including the GitHub repository,
issue tracker, pull requests, documentation discussions, and other public
forums where Epsilon project work is being discussed.
